Privacy Policy

Happy Kid Pediatric Clinic — Marilao, Bulacan
Last Updated: November 21, 2025

We at Happy Kid Pediatric Clinic ("we", "our", "us") respect your privacy and are committed to protecting the personal and medical information of our patients and their families. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information in connection with the healthcare services we provide.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of information:

  • Personal Information: patient and parent/guardian full name, address, phone number, email address, date of birth, and emergency contact details.
  • Medical Information: medical history, diagnoses, allergies, medications, immunization records, diagnostic results, treatment plans, physician notes, and other clinical records.
  • Administrative & Billing Information: insurance provider and policy numbers, billing and payment history, and other information necessary to process claims and payments.

2. How We Collect Information

We collect information through:

  • Patient registration forms (paper or electronic)
  • Medical consultations and examinations
  • Electronic medical records and clinical systems
  • Communications by phone, email, SMS, messaging apps, or our website
  • Insurance claims, laboratory or diagnostic reports, and referrals

3. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information to:

  • Provide and coordinate pediatric healthcare services and treatment;
  • Maintain accurate medical records and medical history;
  • Communicate appointment reminders, test results, care instructions, and health notices;
  • Process insurance claims and payments and perform billing functions;
  • Comply with legal, regulatory, and public health reporting requirements;
  • Perform quality assurance, training, and clinic operations.

4. How We Protect Your Information

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect information against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. Safeguards include access controls, staff training, secure electronic medical record systems, and encrypted storage where appropriate.

5. Sharing and Disclosure

We will only share patient information when necessary and permitted by law or with your consent. Common disclosures include:

  • Other healthcare providers involved in a patient’s care (referrals, specialists, hospitals);
  • Insurance companies for claims adjudication and payment;
  • Diagnostic laboratories and imaging centers for test processing;
  • When required by law, court order, or public health authorities.

We do not sell patient information or share it with third parties for marketing purposes.

6. Rights of Parents and Patients

Subject to applicable law, parents or legal guardians generally have the right to access, request a copy of, or request corrections to their child’s medical records. You may also be able to withdraw consent for certain uses of your information, subject to legal and contractual restrictions. To exercise these rights, please contact us using the details below.

7. Data Retention

We retain medical and administrative records for the period required by applicable laws and as necessary for treatment, billing, and operational needs. After the retention period, we securely delete or anonymize records in accordance with our policies and legal obligations.

8. Use of Digital Platforms

If you communicate with us through our website, social media, or third-party messaging platforms, information you provide may be subject to those platforms’ privacy policies. We take reasonable steps to protect information transmitted via electronic channels but cannot guarantee the security of communications sent over the internet.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. Material changes may be communicated by notice in our clinic or via the contact details we have on file.
